Q: What are convertible bonds?

A: Bonds that can be converted into company stock at a predetermined price. Investors win if the stock goes up, but can demand cash repayment if it doesn't.

Q: Why does dilution matter?

A: When a company issues more shares, each existing share represents less of the company. Your ownership gets "watered down."

Q: What's "volatility arbitrage"?

A: Hedge funds buy convertible bonds (which are cheap when volatility is high) while simultaneously shorting the stock. They profit whether the stock goes up or down â€” as long as there's movement.
