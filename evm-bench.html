OpenAI Drops a New Crypto Tool — And It's a Big Deal for AI Agents

February 18, 2026 · 4 min read

OpenAI and crypto firm Paradigm just released evm-bench. It's a tool that tests how well AI agents handle smart contracts. Here's why you should care.

AI Tools OpenAI Paradigm

OpenAI just dropped something interesting for the crypto world — and it's not a token.

Teaming up with Paradigm (the crypto investment firm behind Uniswap and dozens of other DeFi protocols), they've released evm-bench. That's a tool that measures how well AI agents perform when dealing with smart contracts on Ethereum and EVM-compatible blockchains.

What does it actually do?

Think of it like a driving test — but for AI agents trying to navigate the crypto ecosystem. The tool runs AI models through various scenarios involving:

  • Reading and understanding smart contract code
  • Executing transactions on DeFi protocols
  • Navigating multi-step financial operations
  • Handling edge cases and failures

Why does this matter?

Right now, a lot of "AI agents" in crypto are pretty limited. They can answer questions, summarize docs, maybe execute a simple trade. But actually interacting with complex DeFi protocols? That's harder.

evm-bench gives developers a standardized way to test and improve AI agents for real-world crypto tasks. It's a benchmark — like how ImageNet changed computer vision, this could become the standard for measuring AI in DeFi.

And with crypto trading volumes hitting hundreds of billions daily, the opportunity for AI agents to actually do useful work (not just pump tokens on social media) is huge.

The bigger picture

This collaboration is interesting. OpenAI is the big dog in AI. Paradigm is one of the most respected crypto-native investment firms. When they team up on something, it's worth paying attention to.

Whether this leads to AI agents that can actually manage your DeFi positions, execute sophisticated trading strategies, or spot vulnerabilities in smart contracts — we don't know yet. But having a benchmark to measure progress is the first step.

The question is: how long until your AI assistant can do your DeFi farming for you?
